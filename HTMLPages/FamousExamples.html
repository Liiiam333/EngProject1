Famous Examples

  • Romeo and Juliet

  • Despite the play being written in 1597, Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet, a story about two “star-crossed lovers” who quite literally fall in love with each other at first sight, has become a household name representing said love at first sight. The story also, much like the other two on this list, ends with tragedy, highlighting both the ups and downs of love.

  • Titanic

  • The Titanic, first shown in 1997 (coincidentally, by year, exactly four centuries after Romeo and Juliet), is often considered the most modern and well-known depiction of a love at first sight story. Although in some ways a simple story about two people who meet and find to love each other before disaster strikes (the ship sinking) and only one survives. It represents the sheer impact a well-made story about love can have in modern day, as it held top box office sales for over two decades, is still the fourth highest grossing film nearly three decades later and is only one of two movies produced before the year 2000 to be in the top 100 grossing movies (The Lion King taking place 54).

  • Cleopatra and Mark Antony

  • While the other two examples here are fiction (or historical fiction), one of the oldest examples of love at first sight in the real world was with Cleopatra, queen of Egypt, and Mark Antony, Roman general and politician, from around 40-30 BCE. The two, although joined in some respects politically, came to love each other dearly to the point the two died by suicide after being defeated by Antony’s previous ally Octavian. Shakespeare also had an impact on the story with his own play, though the original story is still evidently very likely to be true.
